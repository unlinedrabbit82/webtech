thoughts on progressive enhancement

Progressive enhancement is building your website for functionality in all browsers, then including more advanced features for the more capable ones. This is important in modern web dev becase with the wide expanse of the internet that we have today, there are many browsers, and most are different from one another. So, if you dont build for variety, you will fall behind and lose that base you could have recieved from those other browsers.

project reflection

During this project I ran into quite a few issues, my first major one was deciding where to start. This took me about a day before I finally got through the major work of writing, Since I wanted to write most of it myself. Another problem I ran into was underestimating the time it took to produce a functional website, I spent a really late night completing the first draft of this project, I learned the hard way to not put Webdesign projects off after that night. One of the things I learned was to appreciate the time and dedication that goes into the websites I browse frequently, as they have hundereds of pages made by people. If I were to do this project over again, I would select something with more narative freedom, I confined myself to a limited selection after chosing to write the main few pages of a writing hub. But, I would still probably choose something around the backrooms, just different content.

Honestly the person I got feedback from didnt give alot of comments, They mostly targeted a few design choices like color, which I either agreed on, or told them no because it went against the them. Or a select few functional tweaks, like helping me decide I wanted to do an accordian for the levels list, which I ultimately agreed on. To do the accordian I found some online resources that semi worked, then I asked an AI to help me with syntax to adjust it slightly, resulting in what I have now.

For the semester, this is the class that has honestly taught me the most out of the many courses I have taken over the past few years. I came in knowing almost nothing about how web design worked, I am leaving with almost a full functional coding kit (minus more advanced javascript). I will be able to turn this knowlege around into making my own websites for portfolios and such. But the biggest thing I found was a curiosity, I will most likely pursue a double major into web design, as long as the cards fall right. I wish to learn a bit more about web design before im done in university.
